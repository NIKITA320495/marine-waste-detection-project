AI-WasteGuard

Deep Learning for Water Body Waste Detection and Classification

Project Overview

The objective of the AI-WasteGuard project is to utilize artificial intelligence and machine learning to address the pervasive problem of waste pollution in water bodies. By employing advanced image classification techniques, the project aims to autonomously detect and classify various types of waste materials present in rivers, lakes, and oceans. This proactive approach will provide environmental authorities with real-time insights into water quality, enabling swift and targeted interventions to mitigate the impact of pollution on aquatic ecosystems and human health.

About Our Project

Forward-Looking Sonar Integration

The application will integrate with forward-looking sonar cameras installed on ships to receive real-time images of underwater environments. These images will serve as input for the detection algorithms.To ensure applicability in diverse environments, the project will exclusively utilize public datasets for training the AI models.

AI-Based Object Detection

Utilizing AI algorithms, particularly deep learning techniques, the application will analyze sonar images to detect various objects, including marine waste and obstructions. The AI models will be trained on public datasets to ensure adaptability to diverse marine environments.

Real-Time Processing

Real-time processing harnesses advanced algorithms to swiftly analyze underwater surroundings, empowering timely intervention for the mitigation of environmental threats like waste accumulation or obstructive materials, thereby safeguarding marine ecosystems.
